# Quick Start Guide - Espresso Tracker

Get started with Espresso Tracker in 5 minutes!

## 📱 Opening the App

When you first open Espresso Tracker, you'll see four tabs at the bottom:
1. **Brew** - Main brewing interface
2. **History** - View past shots
3. **Equipment** - Manage grinders & machines
4. **Beans** - Track your coffee beans

## 🚀 First Time Setup (2 minutes)

### Step 1: Add Your Grinder
1. Tap **Equipment** tab
2. Make sure "Grinders" is selected at the top
3. Tap the **+** button
4. Fill in:
   - Name (e.g., "Niche Zero")
   - Brand (e.g., "Niche")
   - Burr Type (Flat/Conical)
   - Burr Size (e.g., 63mm)
5. Tap **Save**

### Step 2: Add Your Espresso Machine
1. Still in **Equipment** tab
2. Swipe left or tap "Machines" at the top
3. Tap the **+** button
4. Fill in:
   - Name (e.g., "Gaggia Classic")
   - Brand (e.g., "Gaggia")
   - Boiler Type (Single/Double/Heat Exchanger)
5. Tap **Save**

### Step 3: Add Your Coffee Beans
1. Tap **Beans** tab
2. Tap the **+** button
3. Fill in:
   - Name (e.g., "Ethiopia Yirgacheffe")
   - Roaster (e.g., "Local Coffee Roasters")
   - Origin (e.g., "Ethiopia")
   - Roast Date (use date picker)
4. Tap **Save**

**Note**: The app will automatically track bean freshness from the roast date!

## ☕ Brewing Your First Shot

### Step 1: Navigate to Brew Tab
Tap **Brew** at the bottom

### Step 2: Select Equipment
Tap the dropdown menus to select:
- Your grinder
- Your espresso machine
- Your coffee beans

### Step 3: Set Parameters
Fill in:
- **Grind Setting** (e.g., "15")
- **Water Temp** (default: 93°C)
- **Pressure** (default: 9.0 bar)

### Step 4: Choose Brew Ratio
Tap one of the preset ratios:
- **Ristretto** (1:1.5) - Concentrated, intense
- **Normale** (1:2.0) - Standard espresso
- **Lungo** (1:2.5) - Longer extraction
- **Custom** (1:3.0) - Adjust as needed

### Step 5: Enter Dose
Type your input dose in grams (e.g., "18")
The app will automatically calculate target yield based on your ratio.

### Step 6: Pull Your Shot
1. **Start** your espresso machine
2. Tap the **Start** button when you begin extraction
3. The timer will count up in real-time
4. Watch the extraction quality indicator:
   - **Under-extracted** (< 20s) - Orange
   - **Optimal** (20-30s) - Green
   - **Over-extracted** (> 35s) - Red
5. Tap **Stop** when your shot is finished

### Step 7: Finish & Save
1. Tap **Finish & Save Shot**
2. Enter actual **Yield Out** in grams
3. Rate your shot (1-5 stars)
4. Add tasting notes (optional)
5. Add a photo (optional)
6. Tap **Save**

**Done!** Your shot is now saved in History.

## 📊 Viewing Your Progress

### Check Your History
1. Tap **History** tab
2. See statistics at the top:
   - Total shots brewed
   - Average brew time
   - Average ratio
   - Shots this week
3. Scroll to see all your shots
4. Tap any shot to see full details

### View Shot Details
In detail view, you'll see:
- Brewing parameters
- Equipment used
- Bean information
- Extraction analysis
- Your notes and photos

## 💡 Tips & Best Practices

### For Beginners
- Start with the **Normale** (1:2) ratio
- Aim for 25-30 second extraction time
- Adjust grind setting if too fast/slow
- Keep notes on what worked well

### For Consistency
1. **Always fill in grind setting** - Critical for reproducibility
2. **Track your dose** - Use the same dose for consistency
3. **Note water temp** - Affects extraction
4. **Rate every shot** - Helps identify patterns

### Using Bean Freshness
- Check the freshness indicator on bean cards
- **Best results**: 7-21 days from roast
- **Peak flavor**: Usually 10-14 days
- Update bean info if you open a new bag

### Equipment Management
- Add notes about modifications or maintenance
- Track purchase dates for warranty
- Store photos for insurance purposes
- Update notes after any changes

## 🎯 Common Workflows

### Perfect Your Recipe
1. Brew a shot with specific parameters
2. Rate and note the results
3. Adjust one variable (grind, dose, etc.)
4. Brew another shot
5. Compare in History
6. Repeat until perfect!

### Track Bean Performance
1. Select a bean in Brew tab
2. Pull multiple shots over several days
3. Go to Bean detail view
4. See "Total Shots" statistic
5. Remember optimal grind setting in notes

### Compare Equipment
1. Brew with Grinder A
2. Brew with Grinder B (same bean, same parameters)
3. Check History to compare
4. Note which produced better results

## 🆘 Troubleshooting

### Can't see my equipment in Brew tab
- Make sure you've added equipment in Equipment tab
- Check that you saved the equipment (tap Save)

### Timer won't start
- Make sure you've filled in dose
- Try tapping Reset then Start again

### Can't save shot
- Yield Out is required
- Make sure you stopped the timer first

### Images not showing
- Grant photo library access in Settings
- Check storage space on device
